Frequently Asked Questions

Clinical processes, prescriptions, and compliance.

Who evaluates and prescribes the medications?

All patient evaluations and prescriptions are strictly performed by Licensed Florida Healthcare Providers (Doctors and Nurse Practitioners). Our clinical team utilizes their independent medical judgment to determine if a requested therapy is clinically appropriate and safe for every individual patient.

Where do the medications come from?

We work exclusively with fully accredited, U.S.-based pharmacies. Our primary compounding pharmacy partners are Strive Pharmacy (NABP-accredited and LegitScript-certified) and Empower Pharmacy. Medications are accurately formulated in PCAB-accredited 503A facilities subject directly to state Boards of Pharmacy.

Are controlled substances prescribed?

No. Under no circumstances does Patriotic Virtual Telehealth prescribe DEA-Scheduled controlled substances, such as Adderall, Xanax, or Phentermine. Our platform is exclusively focused on weight management via specific GLP-1 therapies, and longevity science.

Is the service legally compliant and secure?

Yes. We strictly follow all state and federal telehealth laws. Patriotic Telehealth utilizes a HIPAA-compliant EMR system, mandatory tele-consultation reviews, and secure e-prescribing tools to ensure the highest standard of patient safety and data privacy.

What is the refund policy?

Because we utilize professional medical time and custom compound medications, all services are strictly non-refundable once an interactive consultation is performed by a licensed provider or a prescription is sent to the pharmacy. This absolute policy is agreed upon prior to checkout. If a provider determines you are simply not eligible for treatment before services are fully rendered, your initial consultation fee will be refunded.

Are medications prescribed automatically?

No. All medications are prescribed only after an evaluation by a licensed healthcare provider. Treatment decisions are based on clinical judgment, patient-specific factors, and established medical guidelines.

Do you use AI to diagnose or treat patients?

No. Patriotic Virtual Telehealth does not use AI to independently diagnose, treat, or prescribe medications. Any technology used serves only as a support tool for providers.

How is artificial intelligence used on the platform?

AI tools may be used to support clinical workflows and provide evidence-based reference information. These tools are designed to assist providers and do not replace clinical decision-making.

Are AI-generated recommendations reviewed by a doctor?

Yes. Any outputs generated by support tools are reviewed and validated by a licensed healthcare provider before being used in patient care.

Is my health information used to train AI systems?

No. Patient data is not used to train AI systems without proper authorization. All data is handled in accordance with HIPAA and applicable privacy laws.

How do you ensure safe prescribing across different states?

Providers only treat patients in states where they are licensed. Patient location is verified, and prescriptions are issued and fulfilled only in compliance with applicable state and federal regulations.

What medications do you offer for weight loss?

Treatment is personalized and determined by a licensed healthcare provider. When clinically appropriate, options may include GLP-1 medications (such as semaglutide in injectable or oral form, or tirzepatide), oral medications such as naltrexone/bupropion (Contrave), and other supportive therapies.

How are medications dosed?

Dosing is individualized based on your medical history, tolerance, and clinical response. Many medications are started at a lower dose and gradually increased to improve tolerability and safety.

Do you use compounded medications?

When appropriate and permitted by law, compounded medications may be prescribed. Patients are informed of available options, including FDA-approved medications.

Where are prescriptions filled?

Prescriptions are fulfilled by licensed U.S. pharmacies. Pharmacy partners independently review prescriptions to ensure compliance with applicable regulations.

Do you offer oral GLP-1 medications?

Yes, when clinically appropriate, oral GLP-1 medications such as semaglutide may be prescribed. Your provider will determine the best formulation based on your needs.
