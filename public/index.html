Dossier 01 - Oral GLP-1 Therapy

A medicine that survives the stomach.

For nearly a century, GLP-1 medications required a needle. A small fatty-acid molecule called SNAC changed that by creating a brief, protected window for absorption directly through the gastric lining.

FDA approval Sep 20191
Active compound Semaglutide
Carrier SNAC2
02The Obstacle

The stomach is built to destroy what semaglutide is made of.

Semaglutide is built from a short chain of amino acids, the same kind of structure the stomach was designed to break down. Hydrochloric acid maintains a pH between 1 and 2, and the enzyme pepsin shreds these amino-acid bonds at peak efficiency in that range.3

Without protection, a swallowed dose would be reduced to fragments before reaching the bloodstream. This is why previous GLP-1 medications required a subcutaneous injection.

How SNAC Works

How SNAC escorts the molecule through.

SNAC (sodium N-[8-(2-hydroxybenzoyl)amino] caprylate) is not a coating or a capsule. It is a small fatty-acid derivative that performs three coordinated actions within a few centimeters of the tablet, just long enough for absorption to happen before it dissolves away.2,4

01a
Stage One - Buffering

Raise the local pH.

As the tablet erodes, it releases roughly 300 mg of SNAC into a small zone against the stomach wall. This creates a concentrated microenvironment that locally raises the pH from around 1-2 to approximately 5. At pH 5, pepsin loses much of its ability to degrade the molecule, and semaglutide stays intact.4,5

FIG. 03 - LOCAL pH SHIFT a - BUFFERING
02b
Stage Two - Monomerization

Separate the clusters.

Semaglutide molecules naturally cluster together. As single molecules, they can cross a cell membrane; as clusters, they are too large. SNAC interferes with these aggregates and shifts the equilibrium toward individual molecules ready for absorption.4

FIG. 04 - AGGREGATE DISSOCIATION b - MONOMERIZATION
03g
Stage Three - Permeation

Cross the epithelium.

SNAC briefly increases the fluidity of the gastric cell membrane, opening a transcellular path for semaglutide. The effect is concentration-dependent and reversible: as SNAC disperses, the membrane returns toward baseline. The result is approximately 1% oral bioavailability, enough because semaglutide is highly potent and persists for about a week.2,4,6

FIG. 05 - TRANSCELLULAR PERMEATION g - ABSORPTION
04The Result

The injection, reformulated.

The same active molecule used in weekly injections, delivered through a daily tablet. A small amount of semaglutide can do meaningful work because the molecule binds tightly to GLP-1 receptors and remains in the body for roughly seven days.2

That long half-life is why even approximately 1% absorption can be therapeutically meaningful. The dose is not smaller. The route is different.

05Clinical Context

An FDA-approved molecule with clinically specific indications.

Oral semaglutide is approved by the FDA for adults with type 2 diabetes to improve glycemic control alongside diet and exercise.7 It is not interchangeable with injectable forms, and weight-related indications for GLP-1 medications vary by formulation. Eligibility, dosing, and monitoring are determined during a clinical consultation.

Approved Use

Type 2 Diabetes

Adjunct to diet and exercise to improve glycemic control in adults with type 2 diabetes mellitus.7

Mechanism

GLP-1 Receptor Agonist

Mimics the body's natural incretin hormone, enhances insulin secretion, slows gastric emptying, and reduces appetite signaling.2

Contraindications

Not for Everyone

Contraindicated in patients with a personal or family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma or MEN 2 syndrome. Boxed warning applies.7

Wegovy®

semaglutide injection · once-weekly GLP-1 therapy for chronic weight management

  • FDA-approved once-weekly subcutaneous injection indicated alongside a reduced-calorie diet and increased physical activity.1

  • In the STEP-1 trial, adults on Wegovy lost a mean of ~14.9% of body weight at 68 weeks, compared to 2.4% on placebo.2

  • Also FDA-approved to reduce the risk of major cardiovascular events in adults with established cardiovascular disease and either obesity or overweight.3

  • Dose is titrated gradually from 0.25 mg to 2.4 mg over ~16 weeks to support tolerability.1

Wegovy is supplied as a single-patient-use, pre-filled, disposable pen with a fixed dose. Each pen contains one weekly dose at the strength prescribed by your clinician: 0.25 mg, 0.5 mg, 1 mg, 1.7 mg, or 2.4 mg.

The needle is short and fine, typically 4 mm and 32 gauge, designed for subcutaneous injection into the abdomen, thigh, or upper arm. Most patients report minimal discomfort once they are comfortable with technique.

PVT clinicians review injection technique with you during your consultation and provide written and video guidance.

Semaglutide is a GLP-1 receptor agonist. It mimics a hormone the body naturally produces after eating, which helps signal fullness, slow gastric emptying, and improve insulin response.

Wegovy is the FDA-approved brand of semaglutide indicated for chronic weight management at the 2.4 mg weekly dose. Other brands of semaglutide, including Ozempic® and Rybelsus®, are approved for type 2 diabetes. Wegovy and Ozempic share the same active molecule but differ in indication and maximum dose.4

Wegovy is injected once weekly on the same day each week, at any time of day, with or without food.1

You start at the lowest dose, 0.25 mg, and increase every four weeks until you reach the maintenance dose of 2.4 mg or the highest dose you tolerate. This gradual ramp-up reduces gastrointestinal side effects.

Injection sites should be rotated. Wegovy must be refrigerated before first use. After first use, pens may be kept at room temperature for up to 28 days.

GLP-1 power, prescribed properly.

Wegovy is one of the most clinically studied weight-management medications available. Here's what the evidence says.

01 / 04

FDA approved

Wegovy is approved by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration as a clinically reviewed treatment for chronic weight management in qualifying adults and adolescents 12+.1

02 / 04

Rooted in biology

Semaglutide acts like your body's own GLP-1 hormone, signaling satiety, slowing gastric emptying, and reducing appetite naturally rather than artificially suppressing it.4

03 / 04

Real results

In the STEP-1 trial of adults with obesity, those on Wegovy lost a mean of 14.9% of body weight at 68 weeks vs. 2.4% on placebo, alongside diet and exercise.2

04 / 04

Heart benefit

Wegovy is also FDA-approved to reduce the risk of cardiovascular death, heart attack, and stroke in adults with established cardiovascular disease and overweight or obesity.3

05Beyond Weight

More than a number on a scale.

Clinical trials have documented effects beyond weight reduction. With sustained use alongside diet and exercise, Wegovy has been associated with:

  • Improvements in blood pressure and waist circumference2
  • Better glycemic control in patients with prediabetes5
  • Reduced risk of major cardiovascular events in eligible adults3
  • Improvements in lipid profile and inflammatory markers2

Individual results vary. Wegovy is intended as part of a comprehensive weight-management plan that includes a reduced-calorie diet and increased physical activity. It is not a substitute for lifestyle change.

06Common Questions

Frequently asked.

Wegovy is FDA-approved for adults with a body mass index (BMI) of 30 or greater, or 27 or greater with at least one weight-related condition such as hypertension, type 2 diabetes, or dyslipidemia. It is also approved for adolescents aged 12 and older with obesity.1

Final eligibility is determined by your PVT clinician during your intake and consultation.

Wegovy is approved as a chronic weight-management medication, not a short-term solution. Clinical evidence suggests that discontinuing treatment can lead to regain of much of the weight lost.2

Most patients are encouraged to view Wegovy as part of a long-term plan. Your clinician will discuss what an ongoing or stepped-down plan might look like based on your progress.

The most common side effects are gastrointestinal: nausea, diarrhea, vomiting, constipation, and abdominal pain. These are most frequent during dose escalation and typically diminish as your body adjusts.1

Serious risks include pancreatitis, gallbladder issues, kidney problems, and a boxed warning for thyroid C-cell tumors. Wegovy is contraindicated in patients with a personal or family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma or Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia syndrome type 2.1

Your PVT clinician will review your full medical history to identify any contraindications before prescribing.

Insurance coverage for Wegovy varies significantly by plan and indication. Some plans cover it for chronic weight management. Others only cover it for the cardiovascular-risk-reduction indication.

PVT is a cash-pay telehealth practice. We provide consultations and prescriptions, but the medication is filled through a pharmacy partner. We can provide documentation for you to submit to your insurance separately.

Switching between GLP-1 medications is common and should always be supervised by a clinician. Your PVT provider will review your prior treatment, current dose, and reason for switching, and design an appropriate transition plan.

Wegovy should never be combined with another semaglutide-containing product or another GLP-1 receptor agonist.1

Wegovy's complete Prescribing Information and Medication Guide are published by Novo Nordisk and the FDA. Your PVT clinician will provide a copy during your consultation and review the most important sections with you.
